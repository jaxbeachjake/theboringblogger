    Shaving Essentials that won't break the bank.

    March 18, 2022 in mens health, hygiene

    Don’t buy in to those witty “shave clubs” or bargain razor brands

    I don’t shave like I used to. Years ago I was in the military and had to have a clean mug on the daily. After that I kept up the routine out of habit and to look “professional”. Now I mainly sport a 5-oclock shadow and keep the lines tight. Any way you cut it, shaving can be expensive, and a pain (both literally, and financially).

    Razor brands have gone from 1 fixed blade to more than I can count. The razor head wobbles and weaves and the blades move in and around in an attempt to contour themselves to any facial shape while still somehow cutting down any follicles in their way.

    It’s gotten expensive too. For the most part razors, and shaving cream, after shave, etc. are all consumables. You’re meant to use them and then throw them away, purchase more, and throw those away, and purchase more etc. etc. ad infinitum.

    Don’t get me started on electric razors and all of the bumps, bruises, and ingrown hairs that can result in using those overpriced hack jobs.

    So about 10 years ago I got fed up with this cycle of poor-performing razors, weird smelling/feeling shaving creams/gels, and the ever increasing price of shaving in general. With a little bit of research I found a much better solution.

    The Problem

    As I mentioned most shaving companies are focused on innovation. Creating new products: razors with more/flexible blades, shaving cream that’s more like lube, and now-a-days the best way for these companies to monetize and make the most bang for their marketing bucks through consumers is the subscription. You feel like you’re getting a no-hassle deal but in reality you’re locked into a monthly/yearly fee for a marginal product. Let’s face it, none of these products are top of the line, and although they may work, and be within your price range, you really could save some money by not falling for the subscription here. So what can you do here?

    How about instead of innovation, we opt for tried and true?

    Shave like your Dad….or maybe your Grandad

    Look at the fancy shaves people pay for in the barber shops. What do they use? A straight razor right? While that may take some practice, and a bit of skill (and I’m not knocking anyone who has it!), the same results can be had with the ole safety razor. Hit up this link for some recommendations. I use a German-made Mercur long handle Barber Pole, which retails for about $60, but another just as good Mercur retails for around half that on Amazon if you want to keep this on a budget, but still sustainable.

    Remember, that’s what this is all about. Keeping the routine sustainable, workable, clean, and even enjoyable. Using a Mercur is a bit heavy, easy to control, and best of all, the razor blades are one size fits all.

    Speaking of blades I use Astra which retail for under $10 on Amazon for a pack of 100. When’s the last time you bought a pack of 100 blades for your razor for under $10? Want to go for longevity? A pack of 300 costs under $25! I honestly can’t remember the last time I bought razor blades, it’s been years!

    So yeah there is some upfront cost with the razor itself, but it’s minimal depending on what you get and in comparison to overall spending for any other Gillette model or shaving club long-term.

    Let’s talk about shaving cream

    So shaving cream is another consumable where you can basically spend as much or as little as you want. A basic staple that (for me anyway) has always worked well is the Italian-based Proraso. It’s soothing/cooling and the razor glides smoothly over it. It’s also ~$10 a tube which makes it affordable for most. Also, a little can go a long way, which is a good thing to take into consideration.
    Now that I don’t shave as much I opt to indulge a bit in this area and use the English-based Taylor of Old Bond Street. It is a bit more expensive for what you get $18 for 5.3oz. that you would in the Proraso tube at 5.2oz. I wouldn’t say it’s altogether a better shaving cream, I just prefer it on occasion. Of course these are just two options out of a myriad of others and your skin type may play a large role in what you can use (keep in mind that both Proraso and Taylor of old Bond Street have options for sensitive skin).

    Now you may disagree, but I’ve always found that with a decent razor and shaving cream that I’ve never been in dire need of an aftershave, but again this maybe up to your individual skin type. The two items I listed above have options for these items if so desired.

    Like anything else, with decent equipment (at a decent price), you can turn a dull routine into a worthwhile endeavor that you can be proud of every morning (or whenever you choose).
